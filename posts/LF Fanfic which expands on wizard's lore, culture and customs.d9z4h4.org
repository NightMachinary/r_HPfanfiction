** Time for the deep dive then:

- linkffn(Forging the Sword): while this fic is usually recommended for the stellar characterizations, there is no denying how effectively it explores the culture and lore of the wizarding world. For example, the conversations with Harry and Ron about how significant magic is to their culture, to the point that giving up a portion of it for non-violence would never be considered (and indeed be offensive), are some of the most nuanced but balanced explorations of canon magic I have yet to see.
- linkffn(Victoria Potter): I cannot say how much I would recommend this fem!Harry story, but Taure has done a masterful job creating a more in depth exploration of Harry Potter, with a high focus on magic and culture. The One He Feared is also another work in this category, though is far shorter.
- linkffn(Bungle in the Jungle: A Harry Potter Adventure): goes into a lot of detail into the application of magic in 'the real world,' this fic definitely shows off new cultures and lore. The 2nd person view is pretty tough to work around, but I found listening to the fic rather than reading helps ease that issue. Warning - mild to moderate bashing depending on character, though this is tempered somewhat later on.
- linkffn(The Phoenix and the Serpent): okay, this is an old one, but dang if the author didn't go into a ton of detail inventing a new (post-GoF) wizarding culture and history. Its incomplete, but the major story arcs end relatively well where it stopped, so I found its not that disjointed (unlike say 0800-Rent-a-Hero).

I will also give shout outs to a few others that you can check up on (since this is already fairly long): Out of the Night by Raining Ink, Black Luminary by YakAge, HP and the Prince of Slytherin by The Sinister Man, An Unfound Door by joe6991, and The Long Journey Home by Rakeesh.

Hope you like /something/ here.
