Read Insidious Inquisitor linkffn(4390267) for the first time today and I'd love more stories where the bad guys aren't so bad after all. A nice side of the usual bashing victims would not go amiss :)


** linkffn(Speak Softly, Love): Dolohov

linkffn(Prince of the Dark Kingdom): depending on your viewpoint, Voldemort and Snape (and possibly Dumbledore).

linkffn(The Phoenix and the Serpent): there is an entire chapter centered on this facet of a character. Its a tearjerker.

linkffn(Harry Potter and the Prince of Slytherin): two characters in particular, but this happens quite a lot with others as well.

linkffn(Hail Odysseus): Grindelwald was short-changed by Dumbledore, decided to train Harry himself.

linkffn(The Prisoner's Cipher): the catch is that Harry is the enemy.

linkffn(Unatoned): basically a dark Daphne as a romance side.

linkffn(To Shape and Change): Snape goes back to get Harry ready to conquer.

Linkffn(On the Way to Greatness): there is a rather believable attempt to show why Slytherins would buy into the pureblood propaganda, if you were in their shoes as well. Not good per se, but its less cartoonish and more believable from the characters.

Linkffn(The Darkness Within): hesitant to recommend this, but Draco and somewhat Voldemort are portrayed in a far more human manner for a variety of reasons. Even Lucius, Bellatrix, Peter, and Narcissa get their moments.
