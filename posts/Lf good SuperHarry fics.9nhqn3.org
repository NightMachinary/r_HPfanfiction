****** u/ParanoidDrone:
#+begin_quote
  I don't think I've read the Lone Traveler... is that part of a series?
#+end_quote

Yes. Very yes. The short version is that Harry fucked up the time travel method from the start of Nightmares of Future Past and now gets pulled against his will to times, places, and universes where he can make a positive difference, then once his job is done he's pulled to the next such place, lather rinse repeat. Because time travel is weird he's recognized as a legendary figure of sorts fairly early on. Eventually he starts entering non-HP universes such as Star Trek or Battlestar Galactica or Buffy the Vampire Slayer. And even more eventually it's slowly explained why this is happening to him in the first place and he accepts his new role.

Harry is hilariously OP and the narration is sometimes a bit preachy (the importance of free will is a major theme throughout the whole thing) but it's entertaining enough to read. Very episodic, easy to read just a chapter or two without feeling like you have to keep going due to cliffhangers. Probably not for everyone but that's true for all stories IMO.
