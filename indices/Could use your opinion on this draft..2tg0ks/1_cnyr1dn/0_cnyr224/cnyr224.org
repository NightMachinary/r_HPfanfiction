  In linguistics, an *absolute construction* is a [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammatical_construction][grammatical construction]] standing apart from a normal or usual syntactical relation with other words or sentence elements. It can be a [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-finite_clause][non-finite clause]] that is [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dependent_clause][subordinate]] in form and modifies an entire sentence, an adjective or possessive pronoun standing alone without a modified [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Substantive][substantive]], or a [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transitive_verb][transitive verb]] when its object is implied but not stated.
